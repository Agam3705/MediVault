Topic: Confidentiality of Patient Medical Records

Purpose: Designing a secure system for managing and protecting patient medical records

1. Project Overview

MediVault is a role-based, secure web application built in Laravel that digitizes and protects patient medical records. It is designed so that real patients can own their health data, doctors can access it only with consent, and every single interaction leaves a traceable audit trail.

1.1 The Problem Being Solved

In the real world — especially in India — medical records are fragmented, paper-based, or siloed inside individual hospitals. When a patient visits a new doctor or has an emergency, critical history is unavailable. There is no consent mechanism, no audit trail, and no patient ownership of their own data. MediVault directly addresses all three.

3.1 Authentication System

Feature

Full registration, login, logout, password reset, and email verification using Laravel Breeze as the scaffolding base.

What it includes

Registration form: name, email, password, role selection (Patient or Doctor)

Login with remember-me toggle

Password reset via email (queued job + Resend/Mailtrap)

Email verification — user must verify before accessing any dashboard

Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity (configurable in settings table)

Last login timestamp recorded in users table on every successful login

Purpose & Real-World Impact

Identity is the foundation of confidentiality. If you cannot trust who is logged in, no other security measure matters. Email verification prevents fake accounts from requesting access to real patient data.

3.2 Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Feature

Three roles — Patient, Doctor, Admin — each with a distinct dashboard, route group, and permission set. Implemented using Spatie Laravel Permission.

What it includes

Middleware groups: patient.only, doctor.only, admin.only applied to all routes

Each role lands on a completely different dashboard after login

Policies (RecordPolicy, AccessGrantPolicy) gate every sensitive action — not just routes

Admin role is seeded in deployment; cannot be self-registered

Role is stamped on every audit log entry

Purpose

RBAC is the technical manifestation of your project's thesis — confidentiality. A doctor cannot stumble onto a record. A patient cannot accidentally edit another patient's file. Every action is gated at the controller AND policy layer.

3.5 Doctor Access Request & Patient Consent System

Feature

The most important feature. No doctor can view any patient record without an explicit, logged consent chain.

The Full Flow

Doctor searches for a patient by name AND one more identifier (DOB or phone)

Doctor submits an access request with a reason (e.g., 'Cardiology consultation')

Patient receives an in-app notification AND email — 'Dr. Sharma has requested access to your records'

Patient reviews the request and clicks Approve or Deny with an optional note

If approved: an access_grant row is created with a default expiry (30 days, configurable)

Doctor can now view all records for that patient until expiry

Patient can revoke access at any time from their Access Control panel

All steps above are individually logged in audit_logs

Access Request Auto-Expiry

If the patient does not respond within 48 hours, the request status changes to 'expired' automatically via a scheduled Laravel job. This prevents requests from lingering indefinitely.

Access Grant Expiry

Access grants have an expires_at timestamp. Another scheduled job runs daily and marks expired grants inactive. Doctors receive an email 3 days before their access expires so they can request a renewal.

Access Types

Read-Only — doctor can view and download records but cannot add or edit

Read-Write — doctor can also add consultation notes and new records for that patient

Purpose

This is the consent model inspired by India's Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission (ABDM). In real health systems, this kind of explicit consent trail is not optional — it is legally required. Your implementation demonstrates an understanding of real-world compliance.

Feature

Account is placed in 'Pending Verification' state — doctor sees a holding screen

Admin can Approve (sets verified_at timestamp) or Reject (with reason, triggers email)

Approved doctor receives a welcome email and gains access to doctor features

Specialization

Current Hospital / Clinic Name

4.3 QR Emergency Card

Feature

Every patient can generate a public QR code that, when scanned by anyone (no login required), displays only the critical health information needed in an emergency. This is the most visually impactful feature of the entire application.

What the Emergency Card Shows

--- TABLE START ---
Field | Why It's On the Card
Full Name + Photo | Identity confirmation
Date of Birth + Age | Dosage calculations, context
Blood Group | Transfusion compatibility — could save a life
Known Allergies | Prevents administration of lethal medication
Chronic Conditions | e.g., Diabetic, Epileptic — changes treatment
Current Critical Medications | Drug interaction prevention
Emergency Contact Name + Phone | Who to call immediately
QR Scan Timestamp Note | Each scan is logged (patient is notified)
--- TABLE END ---

Security Design

The QR URL contains a unique hashed token (not the patient's ID) — not guessable

Patient can regenerate the token at any time, invalidating old QR codes

Patient can toggle the card ON or OFF from their dashboard

Every scan (public, no login) is logged and the patient is notified via email

The card shows ONLY the fields the patient explicitly selects to include

Patient Dashboard Controls

Toggle emergency card on/off

Select which fields to show/hide on the card

View scan history: timestamp, IP, approximate location

Download QR as PNG for printing on a physical wallet card

Purpose

This feature demonstrates that privacy and lifesaving access are not opposites — they can coexist with careful design. A patient who is unconscious and alone cannot consent to sharing their data, yet withholding blood type and allergy information could kill them. The QR card solves this.

4.4 Active Session Management

Feature

Users can see all their currently active sessions (browser + device + IP) and remotely terminate any session they do not recognize.

What It Shows

Device type (Desktop / Mobile / Tablet — detected from User-Agent)

Browser name and OS

IP address

Login time and last active time

Location (city-level from IP geolocation — free API)

Current badge on the active session

Actions

Kill a specific session — immediately invalidates that session token

Kill all other sessions — logs out from every device except the current one

Each kill action is logged in audit_logs and triggers an email alert

Purpose

This is a feature users recognize from Google and banking apps. Its presence signals that the application treats security as a first-class concern, not an afterthought. For a medical app where a leaked account exposes sensitive health data, this is not optional — it is essential.

4.5 Per-Record Visibility Toggle

Feature

When a patient grants a doctor access, that doctor by default sees all records. But patients can hide specific records from specific doctors without revoking the entire access grant.

How It Works

On the Access Grants page, each grant has a 'Manage Visibility' option

Patient sees a checklist of their records with toggles per doctor

Hidden records are excluded from that doctor's record list silently — no error shown

Admin and audit log still show all records regardless of visibility

Purpose

A patient may share records with a cardiologist but want to hide a psychiatric history that is irrelevant to that consultation. Per-record visibility gives patients fine-grained control — the foundation of true data ownership.

4.6 Record Version History

Feature

Every edit to a medical record creates a snapshot of the previous version. No edit ever destroys old data.

What Is Captured

Full JSON snapshot of the record at the time of edit

Who made the change (doctor or patient)

A change note (required — doctor must explain what they changed and why)

Timestamp

Patient Access

Patients can view the full version history of any record. They can see exactly what a doctor changed, when, and why. If a patient believes a change was inappropriate, the history provides an unalterable record for dispute resolution.

Implementation

A Laravel Observer on the Record model fires on the updating event, capturing the pre-update snapshot and writing it to record_versions before saving the new state.

6. Security Edge Cases

These are specific attack vectors and real-world failure modes that the application must handle. Addressing them is what separates a production-grade application from a student project.

6.1 Brute Force Login Protection

The Risk

An attacker tries thousands of password combinations against a known email address.

The Mitigation

Laravel's built-in throttle middleware: max 5 failed attempts in 60 seconds, then 1-minute lockout

After 10 cumulative failures: account temporarily locked, security email sent to the account owner

Failed login attempts are logged in audit_logs with IP address

CAPTCHA option on login form (hCaptcha — free, privacy-respecting)

6.2 Suspicious Session / Unusual Access Detection

The Risk

A legitimate user's session token is stolen (e.g., via a shared computer). The attacker now has full access without knowing the password.

The Mitigation

IP address is recorded at login and compared on subsequent sensitive requests

If IP changes mid-session: user is shown a warning and asked to re-authenticate

User-agent mismatch (browser fingerprint changes): same warning

Active sessions page lets users see and kill any unrecognized session

All of the above is logged in audit_logs with flag unusual_activity = true

6.3 File Upload Security

The Risk

A malicious user uploads a PHP file disguised as a PDF, or a file containing malware, or an oversized file to exhaust storage.

The Mitigation

Server-side MIME type validation (not just file extension) — use PHP's finfo_file()

File extension whitelist: only .pdf, .jpg, .jpeg, .png, .webp

Max file size: 10 MB enforced server-side regardless of client-side validation

Files are stored on Cloudinary with hashed names — never in /public folder

Files served only through an authenticated controller that checks access permissions before returning the file URL

File URLs from Cloudinary are signed/private — not directly accessible without the signed URL

6.4 Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR) Prevention

The Risk

A logged-in doctor changes the record ID in the URL from /records/45 to /records/46 and accesses another patient's record they were never granted access to.

The Mitigation

All record views go through RecordPolicy which checks: does this user have an active, non-expired access_grant for the patient who owns this record?

Laravel's authorize() method is called at the top of every controller action

Returning a 403 (not 404) so unauthorized access is distinguishable in logs from not-found

The access check is at the Policy layer — not just the route level — so it cannot be bypassed by direct controller calls

6.5 Mass Assignment Protection

The Risk

A malicious form submission includes extra fields like role=admin or verified_at=now, which Laravel might mass-assign into the model.

The Mitigation

Every Model has a strict $fillable array — only explicitly listed fields can be mass assigned

Sensitive fields (role, verified_at, is_active) are in $guarded and can only be set via explicit assignment

Form Request classes validate and filter all incoming data before it reaches the controller

6.6 SQL Injection & XSS

The Mitigation

Laravel's Eloquent ORM uses parameterized queries by default — SQL injection is not possible through Eloquent

All output in Blade templates uses {{ }} (escaped) not {!! !!} (raw) — XSS is prevented by default

Content Security Policy headers added via middleware to prevent inline script injection

Any user-generated content rendered as HTML (e.g., consultation notes) is sanitized using HTMLPurifier

6.7 Admin Force-Revoke (Security Incident Response)

The Risk

A doctor's account credentials are compromised. The attacker now has access to every patient who ever granted that doctor access.

The Mitigation

Admin dashboard has a 'Suspend Doctor' action — one click sets is_active = false on the user and is_active = false on all their access_grants

All active sessions for that user are immediately invalidated

All patients who had active grants with this doctor receive an email notification

The entire action is logged with admin_id, doctor_id, and timestamp

Suspended doctor is shown a locked screen on next login with a contact email for reinstatement

8. General Application Features

These are the baseline features every professional web application needs, regardless of domain. They ensure the application feels complete and polished.

8.1 Responsive Design

Built mobile-first using Tailwind CSS

All dashboards, forms, and tables are usable on mobile screens (375px+)

Navigation collapses to a hamburger menu on small screens

File upload area is touch-friendly

8.2 Search & Filter

Patient dashboard: search their own records by title, type, or date range

Doctor dashboard: filter their patient list by name or last access date

Admin panel: search users by name, email, or role. Filter doctors by verification status.

Audit log: filter by action type, date range, user, or target record

8.3 Pagination

All lists (records, audit logs, patients, access requests) are paginated. Default 15 items per page, configurable. Prevents performance issues as data grows.

8.4 Flash Messages & Validation Feedback

All form submissions show success (green) or error (red) flash messages

Inline validation errors appear below each field immediately on submit

Destructive actions (delete, revoke, kill session) require a confirmation dialog

8.5 404, 403, 500 Error Pages

Custom 404: 'Page not found' with a link back to dashboard

Custom 403: 'You do not have permission to view this' — includes who to contact

Custom 500: 'Something went wrong on our end' — with error ID for support reference

8.6 Account Settings

Change email (requires re-verification)

Change password (requires entering current password)

Update profile information

Notification preferences toggle (email on/off per event type)

Danger zone: Deactivate account (soft, not delete)

8.7 Dark / Light Mode Toggle

Tailwind's dark mode class strategy with Alpine.js toggle. Preference stored in localStorage. Small feature, high visual impact — evaluators notice this immediately.

8.8 Localization Readiness

All user-facing strings are stored in Laravel language files (resources/lang/en/) rather than hardcoded in Blade templates. This makes adding Hindi or another language a matter of adding a translation file — not rewriting every view.

12.1 Social Login — Google OAuth

Feature

Users can register and log in using their existing Google account with a single click, eliminating the need to remember a separate password for MediVault.

How It Works

User clicks 'Continue with Google' on the login or register page

Redirected to Google's OAuth consent screen — they approve once

On return, Laravel Socialite retrieves the user's name, email, and Google avatar

If email already exists in the system: linked automatically, user is logged in

If new email: account is created, user is asked to select a role (Patient or Doctor) and complete their profile

A social_accounts table stores the provider + provider_user_id separately so one user can have both email/password AND Google login

New Table: social_accounts

--- TABLE START ---
Column | Type | Purpose
id | bigint PK | Primary key
user_id | FK → users | Links to the MediVault user
provider | varchar | 'google' (extensible to GitHub, Apple, etc.)
provider_user_id | varchar | Google's unique ID for this user
avatar_url | varchar nullable | Google profile photo URL — used as avatar if no custom upload
created_at | timestamp | When the link was established
--- TABLE END ---

Security Notes

Email verification is considered complete for OAuth accounts — Google already verified the email

If an OAuth user wants to set a password later, they can do so from Account Settings

Doctors registered via Google still go through the Admin verification queue — social login does not bypass credentialing

Every OAuth login is recorded in audit_logs with provider: 'google' noted

Package

Laravel Socialite — official first-party package, free. Google OAuth credentials from Google Cloud Console (free tier, unlimited users).

12.2 Persistent User Preferences (Cookie & LocalStorage Layer)

Feature

The application remembers how each user prefers to use it — across sessions and devices — without requiring them to re-configure anything after every login.

What Gets Persisted

--- TABLE START ---
Preference | Storage Method | Behaviour
Dark / Light mode choice | localStorage + cookie | Applied instantly on page load before any render — no flash of wrong theme
Records list view (grid vs list) | localStorage | Remembered per browser — patients who prefer grid always get grid
Records sort order (newest / oldest / type) | localStorage | Last-used sort is restored on next visit
Sidebar collapsed / expanded state | localStorage | Power users who collapse the nav stay collapsed
Notification preferences (email on/off per type) | Database (user_settings table) | Synced across devices — changing on mobile reflects on desktop
Records per page (15 / 30 / 50) | Cookie (30 days) | Pagination preference survives sessions
Last visited section / tab | sessionStorage | Returning to dashboard reopens the last active tab
Language preference (for future i18n) | Cookie + database | Set once, remembered forever
--- TABLE END ---

Cookie Consent Banner

A minimal, non-intrusive banner appears on first visit — one line of text, two buttons: 'Accept' and 'Preferences'. Clicking Accept sets a cookie_consent=true cookie (1-year expiry). Only functional cookies (session, CSRF, preferences) are ever set — no tracking or analytics cookies. This is GDPR-aligned and legally clean.

New Table: user_settings

Stores per-user preferences that must survive across devices (cannot rely on localStorage for these):

user_id, key, value, updated_at — a simple key-value store per user

12.5 Smart Global Search

Feature

A keyboard shortcut (Ctrl+K / Cmd+K) opens a command palette — a floating search overlay that searches across records, doctors, audit events, and settings simultaneously. Results appear in real time as the user types.

What It Searches

Patient: their own records by title, type, description, or date

Doctor: their patient list and record titles for patients they have access to

Admin: users by name/email, doctors by license number, audit events by action type

Search Result Categories

Records — shows type icon, title, date. Click to open the record.

People — (Admin only) shows user name, role, status. Click to go to their profile.

Actions — e.g. typing 'upload' highlights 'Upload a New Record'. Keyboard-navigable shortcuts.

Recent — last 5 items the user visited, shown when the palette opens with no query

Implementation

Built with Alpine.js for the overlay and keyboard interaction. Search queries hit a Laravel controller endpoint that returns grouped JSON results. Debounced at 300ms — no request fires until the user pauses typing. No external search service needed — pure Eloquent full-text search on indexed columns.

Patient Onboarding Steps

Onboarding state tracked via a user_settings key: onboarding_completed=false. Once all steps are done it flips to true and the flow never appears again. Users can skip and return via a 'Complete Setup' banner on their dashboard.

12.7 Skeleton Loaders & Progressive UI

Feature

While data is loading — especially on slower mobile connections — the app shows skeleton screens (animated gray placeholder shapes) instead of a blank white page or a spinning circle.

Where Skeleton Loaders Appear

Records masonry grid — placeholder cards in the correct grid positions

Notification drawer — placeholder rows while fetching

Patient profile sidebar — gray oval for photo, gray lines for name/details

Audit log table — shimmer rows while the query runs

Why It Matters

Skeleton loaders reduce perceived load time by 20–40% in user studies. More importantly for a medical app: they prevent users from thinking the system is broken and clicking away or submitting forms twice. This is especially important on slow connections where a blank screen causes anxiety.

Implementation

Pure CSS with a shimmer animation (background-position keyframe). No JavaScript needed. Blade components with a loading slot: the skeleton shows until an Alpine.js x-show condition flips when the data arrives via a fetch call or Livewire update.

12.8 Drag & Drop File Upload with Preview

Feature

The file upload area accepts drag-and-drop from the desktop. Users can drag a PDF or image directly onto the upload zone without clicking 'Browse'. A live preview appears immediately before they submit.

Behaviour

Dashed drop zone with icon and text: 'Drop your file here or click to browse'

On hover/drag-over: border turns solid blue, background turns light blue — visual confirmation

On drop: file name, size, and type appear below the zone

If file is an image (JPG/PNG): a thumbnail preview renders instantly using FileReader API

If file is a PDF: a PDF icon with the file name and page count (if readable)

If file is too large or wrong type: a red error message appears inline, the file is rejected before upload

Submit button stays disabled until a valid file is selected — prevents empty submissions

Mobile Behaviour

On mobile, the drag-and-drop zone renders as a large tap target that opens the native file/camera picker. The same preview logic applies after selection.

Feature

Patients and doctors can pin up to 5 records to the top of their dashboard for quick access. Pinned records appear in a dedicated 'Pinned' section above the main grid with a subtle pin icon.

Use Cases

Patient pins their most recent prescription so it's always one tap away

Doctor pins a patient's critical allergy record as a constant reminder during consultation

Patient pins their blood test results they need to share repeatedly

12.11 Accessibility (A11y) Foundations

Why This Matters

A medical records system serves people of all abilities, including those with visual impairments, motor limitations, or cognitive challenges. Basic accessibility is not optional in a health application.

What Is Implemented

All interactive elements (buttons, links, inputs) have visible focus rings — keyboard navigation works fully

All images and icons have descriptive alt text or aria-label attributes

Color is never the only indicator of state — critical badges also use text and icons

Form error messages are linked to their fields via aria-describedby so screen readers read them

Modal dialogs trap focus when open and return focus to the trigger when closed

ARIA live regions on notification count and toast messages — screen readers announce updates

Font sizes are rem-based — user's browser font size preference is respected

Minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 on all text (WCAG AA compliance target)

12.13 Progressive Web App (PWA) — Basic

Feature

Users on mobile can add MediVault to their home screen like a native app. When opened from the home screen, it runs without the browser address bar — full-screen, app-like experience.

What This Requires

A web app manifest file (manifest.json): app name, icons, theme color, display: standalone

A service worker for offline caching of the app shell (not medical data — that always requires a connection)

HTTPS — already provided by Railway

App icons at 192px and 512px (designed in Figma or Canva — free)

What Works Offline

The app shell (navigation, layout) loads from cache — no blank screen on slow connections

Previously viewed records are not cached — medical data must always be fresh and authenticated

Offline banner: 'You are offline. Connect to view your records.' — clear, not silent failure

Why Include This

For a medical app targeting India, where many users are on mobile and connections are intermittent, PWA support is genuinely valuable. It also demonstrates awareness of modern web standards beyond basic Laravel.

12.14 Additional Thoughtful Features

Breadcrumb Navigation

Every page shows a breadcrumb trail (e.g., Dashboard > My Records > Blood Test Report — Mar 2025). Eliminates confusion about where the user is, especially after navigating through the audit log or version history.

Contextual Help Tooltips

Small '?' icons next to technical terms (e.g., 'Access Grant', 'Audit Log', 'Critical Record') open a tooltip with a one-sentence plain-language explanation. Medical records systems have unfamiliar vocabulary — tooltips reduce support burden.

Confirmation Dialogs with Consequence Preview

Destructive actions (revoke access, archive record, kill session) show a dialog that explains exactly what will happen: 'Revoking access will immediately prevent Dr. Sharma from viewing any of your records. They will be notified.' Users make informed decisions, not accidental ones.

Time-Relative Timestamps

Dates throughout the app show relative time where appropriate: '2 hours ago', 'Yesterday', '3 days ago' — with the exact date and time in a tooltip on hover. This matches how humans naturally think about recency, especially for audit log entries.

Empty States with Guidance

Every list in the app has a custom empty state — not a blank page. Each empty state has an illustration, a one-line explanation of why it's empty, and a direct call-to-action. For example: no access requests shows 'You have no pending access requests. Search for a patient to request access.' with a search button.

Keyboard Shortcuts Reference

A keyboard shortcuts panel (triggered by pressing '?' anywhere in the app) lists all available shortcuts. Power users — particularly doctors who use the system frequently — will appreciate this. Shortcuts include: Ctrl+K (search), Ctrl+N (new record), Escape (close modal), Arrow keys (navigate list).

'Last Updated' Freshness Indicator

Each record card shows when it was last updated and by whom. If a record has not been updated in over 1 year, a subtle amber 'May be outdated' badge appears. This nudges patients to keep their records current — a real health safety concern.

Copy to Clipboard

One-click copy for shareable links, QR token URLs, and patient IDs. A checkmark animation confirms the copy succeeded. Eliminates the manual select-and-copy dance that breaks on mobile.
